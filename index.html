I spent the last training cycle building a practical foundation in industrial safety — not from a textbook alone, but through eight hands-on modules at Qanmos Center of Excellence, each one focused on a different way a worksite can go wrong and how to stop it before it does.

That means I can step onto a site and already know how a Permit to Work should move through the right hands, how to hold a fire watch or a hole watch without losing focus over a long shift, how to check the air before anyone enters a confined space, and how to respond in the first sixty seconds after someone gets hurt.

I'm early in my career, and I say that plainly rather than dressing it up. What I bring instead is discipline, a genuine respect for procedure, and the eagerness to be the person a site supervisor doesn't have to double-check.

"Looking to join a construction, oil & gas, manufacturing, or industrial team where I can put this training to work and keep learning from people who've done the job longer than I have."
